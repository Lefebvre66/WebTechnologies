Keynote Speakers

KEYNOTE 1: WIRELESS SENSOR NETWORKS AND IOT FOR UNDERWATER MONITORING

DATE: DECEMBER 14, 2025

Speaker: Prof. Jaime Lloret (Polytechnic University of Valencia, Spain)

Prof. Lloret received his B.Sc.+M.Sc. in Physics in 1997, B.Sc.+M.Sc. in Electronic Engineering in 2003, and Ph.D. in Telecommunication Engineering in 2006. He is a Cisco Certified Network Professional Instructor and holds multiple certifications including Hewlett-Packard IT Architect Certification. He is Full Professor at the Polytechnic University of Valencia and Chair of the Integrated Management Coastal Research Institute (IGIC). He founded the "Communications and Networks" research group and heads the "Active and Collaborative Techniques" Innovation Group (EITACURTE).

Prof. Lloret has authored over 1000 research papers (over 600 indexed in Clarivate Analytics JCR) and 15 books, served as editor-in-chief or associate editor for numerous journals, and chaired or co-chaired over 90 international conferences. His work spans telecommunications, sensor networks, IoT, and cognitive systems. He is recognized among the world’s top 2% scientists according to Stanford University (since 2019) and is an IEEE Senior Member, ACM Senior Member, IARIA Fellow, and EAI Fellow.

Keynote 2: Electromagnetics to Empower Future Cognitive Environments

DATE: DECEMBER 15, 2025

Speaker: Prof. Francisco Falcone (Public University of Navarre, Spain)

Prof. Francisco Falcone is a Telecommunications Engineer (1999) and Doctor in Communications (2005), both from the Public University of Navarre (UPNA). He began his career as a Microwave Network Engineer at Siemens-Italtel (1999–2000) and later worked as a Mobile Access Network Engineer at Telefónica Móviles (2000–2008). In 2009, he co-founded Tafco Metawireless, a spin-off of the UPNA.

Prof. Falcone was an Assistant Lecturer at UPNA (2003–2009), Associate Professor (2009–2022), and has been Full Professor since 2022. He is affiliated with the nstitute of Smart Cities (ISC)-UPNA and has been Head of the Institute since May 2021. Since June 2022, he has been a distinguished Visiting Professor at the Telecommunications School of Engineering and Science, Tecnologico de Monterrey, Mexico. His research scope is in Applied and Computational Electromagnetics, with applications in device and system level design in implememtation for context aware and cognitive scenarios supported by wireless communication systems. He has participated in over 110 public and private funded projects.

Keynote 3: Hybrid Quantum Machine Learning and Optimization for 6G Networks

DATE: DECEMBER 16, 2025

Speaker: Dr. Trung Q. Duong (IEEE Fellow, IET Fellow, EIC Fellow, CAE Fellow, AAIA Fellow)

Quantum computing uses the concept of quantum mechanics to offer a massive leap forward in solving complex computation problems. Hybrid quantum-classical machine learning algorithms can significantly enhance processing efficiency and computational speed, making them capable of guaranteeing high QoS requirements of 6G networks. This talk presents the state-of-the-art in quantum machine learning and optimization, providing a comprehensive overview of its potential via machine learning approaches. Furthermore, it introduces quantum-inspired machine learning and optimization applications for 6G networks in terms of 6G channel estimation and RF fingerprinting, highlighting enabling technologies and potential challenges. Finally, it outlines key research issues and future directions for quantum-inspired optimization in 6G networks.

About the Speaker: Dr. Trung Q. Duong is a Canada Excellence Research Chair and Full Professor at Memorial University of Newfoundland, Canada. He is also an Adjunct Professor at Queen’s University Belfast, UK, and a Visiting Professor at Kyung Hee University, South Korea. His research interests include quantum optimization and machine learning in wireless communications. He has authored or co-authored over 650 publications with more than 24,000 citations (h-index 84). Dr. Duong has served as an editor for multiple IEEE journals and received numerous Best Paper Awards, including from IEEE ICC and GLOBECOM. He is currently the Editor-in-Chief of IEEE Communications Surveys & Tutorials and serves as an IEEE ComSoc Distinguished Lecturer. He is a Fellow of IEEE, IET, CAE, EIC, and AAIA, and the Founding Director of the Quantum Communications and Computing Center (QC3).
